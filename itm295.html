ITM 295 Portfolio Service Project

Engage students in service-learning opportunities to bless other lives by using the skills and knowledge gained through their course of study in their degree program.

Why a Service Project?

Benefits & Purpose

Do you like to do nice things for people? Do like technology? Can you learn by helping others? If you answer yes to these questions, this is where you can gain college credit for it as well as practice using service opportunities to build your professional network.

Create a portfolio of work that you can use to "show and tell" to hiring managers in job interview situations.

How to Get Started

Several students have discovered the power of social networking and professional networking by creating and sharing a post and then engaging in conversation until the right opportunity opens up.

Non-profit and public service organizations (like schools and government organizations) make this whole process much simpler for everyone.

Learning Outcomes

Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)

  1. Become a disciple of Jesus Christ through service
  2. Identify and act on opportunities to put technical knowledge and skills into practice in a real-world environment
  3. Develop skills for project planning, scheduling, and overcoming obstacles by seeking help from others
  4. Work with clients and cultivate professional relationships
  5. Cultivate professional peer relationships and become a part of a professional community within an organization
  6. Gain institutional awareness and refine industry professionalism expectations

Project Proposal Requirements

Short Version

To register for the class, you will need to submit two documents:

  1. Write a proposal and receive an authorization from the course instructor.
  2. Have your project beneficiary (the organization and/or person benefiting from the service) acknowledge compliance with applicable labor laws (in the United States, they can submit the ITM 295R Attestation for No Compensation).

Key Requirements

  • Minimum 39 Hours: IT-related volunteer service opportunity
  • Service Focus: Must engage you in semester-long service project
  • Skills Application: Use technical knowledge from degree program
  • Documentation: Weekly journals and self-assessments required

Grading Distribution

Component Percentage
Total Service Hours 50%
Weekly Journal 30%
Weekly Self-Assessment 10%
Closure Meeting, Presentation, Meeting with Instructor 10%
Total 100%

Final Presentation Requirements

  • Prepare a 5-10 minute presentation of your service project
  • Include visual aids and project outcomes
  • Reflect on personal growth and service impact
  • Schedule with instructor two weeks before semester end

Project Examples

Examples of volunteer service performed by students in previous semesters

Coding for Kids Instructor

Serve as an assistant instructor for "Coding for Kids" at a public library where children ages 8 through 12 learn programming concepts using Scratch.

IoT Sensor Module

Build a prototype sensor module which monitors and manages a saltwater coral reef tank.

Assistant SysAdmin

Serve as an assistant sysadmin at a small but growing IT company in California.

Online Presence Consultant

Provide consultation to increase online presence for a cable manufacturing company.

IT Support Technician

Serve as an IT technician to assist the owner of a local electronics and appliance store.
